Flagship use case · Standard or Embedded engagement

Medical Researcher / Principal Investigator

Profile: PI, clinical investigator, post-doc, or research-track physician. Inbox accumulates 200+ messages/day across journal alerts, NIH/sponsor communications, IRB notices, society newsletters, trainee updates, manuscript reviews, clinical-trial coordinator threads. HIPAA-aware by institutional default.

The two-hour daily problem. Pre-system: 90 minutes of newsletter / journal-alert skimming first thing in the morning, another 30 minutes of inbox triage between clinic and bench. Most of it is mechanical — categorize, file, or skim-and-discard. None of it is research.

Post-system outcome — newsletter digest is the killer feature. All 30+ subscription feeds collapse into one 7 AM digest with 80–120 article cards organized by source. Daily time recovered: 60–90 minutes, back into the day. The clinician scans the digest with their first coffee, clicks through to the 5–8 articles actually worth reading, archives the rest by closing the tab.

Plus the rest of the stack. Sponsor mail labeled sponsor + drafted reply waiting for review. IRB notices auto-filed into a dedicated folder. Trainee questions routed to the daily catch-up window. Triage-activity digest at end-of-day shows what the system did with the inbox — no "what is the AI doing while I'm not looking" anxiety. HIPAA mode flagged on the patient-correspondence account for institutional review.

Total recovered: 1.5–2 hours/day, with full transparency over what the system handled and what's waiting for personal attention.

Confidentiality & privilege · Standard or Embedded engagement

Law Firm / Legal Department (Privileged Inbox)

Context: A litigation boutique, in-house legal team, or solo practitioner whose inbox is a running record of privileged client communications and attorney work-product. The volume is real — client threads, opposing counsel, court notices, e-filing alerts — but the sharper concern is where the mail goes the moment an AI reads it.

The problem with routing privileged mail through cloud AI. Send a privileged inbox through a hosted, third-party AI service and you have handed a client's confidential communications to an outside party that now received them. That outside party is a new custodian a subpoena can reach in discovery, and its receipt of the mail is a disclosure the other side can point to when arguing the privilege was waived. The convenience of a hosted model quietly widens the circle of who holds the file.

Outcome — the AI stays inside the firm's walls. The classifier runs on the firm's own hardware; privileged mail and work-product are read and sorted on-box and never transit an outside model. There is no third-party AI vendor to name in a subpoena and no external disclosure to build a waiver argument from — the mail stays under the firm's sole control, the way the file room already does. Sensitive-matter routing, drafted replies held for a lawyer's review (never auto-sent), and a tamper-evident log of who opened which matter round it out.

This is an infrastructure-and-strategy posture, not legal advice — confirm the privilege and discovery implications for your jurisdiction with your own counsel.

Standard engagement · HIPAA mode

Solo Practitioner Clinic (1–5 person practice)

Context: M365 or Google Workspace-based. HIPAA-regulated by default. Need to demonstrate compliance posture to insurers + auditors.

Outcome: HIPAA-mode accounts with PHI-scrubbed logs. Audit chain on every staff access. Drafted patient-correspondence replies for clinician review (never auto-sent). Delegate access for front-desk staff to triage but not draft. Quarterly compliance review packet produced from the audit log.

Embedded engagement · self-use

Fractional CTO / Consulting Firm

Context: Multiple client mailboxes (separately authenticated). Need to keep client communications isolated for confidentiality. Calendar coordination across 8–12 active clients per week.

Outcome: Per-client account with isolated rules and routes. Per-client writing-style profile so responses sound right for each engagement (the assistant's "voice" matches the engagement). Meeting-request intercept on every account with the right working-hours config per client. Watch rules escalate "fire-drill" client emails to SMS instantly.

This is how I run my own consulting practice.

Standard engagement · multi-user

Researcher Cohort Tooling

Context: A lab with N researchers all using shared mail infrastructure. Each researcher has their own writing style and their own preferences. Privacy: one researcher shouldn't see another's mail or drafts.

Outcome: Multi-user install with role-based access. Each researcher gets their own dashboard, their own categories, their own digest. Lab PI has delegate access to specific researcher mailboxes (with audit-row stamping). One Ollama host serves the whole lab; one container, one TLS cert, one backup.

Self-hosted · no engagement needed

Privacy-First Homelab Operator

Context: A self-hosting enthusiast who wants to run everything on their own machines. (For the technically-inclined: containers on Podman, reached over a Tailscale mesh, with the AI model already running locally on their own GPU via Ollama.)

Outcome: Install + configure independently from the public repository at github.com/Unlimited-Data-Works-LLC/Email-Triage. Pull the cosign-signed image from ghcr.io/unlimited-data-works-llc/email-triage, verify the SLSA-3 provenance, run on Podman with a systemd quadlet. Air-gap installable via the hash-pinned embedding-runtime tarball — same verification path as the auto-download. Audit chain and HIPAA-mode features work exactly the same as in any other deployment — the privacy posture isn't gated behind an engagement.
